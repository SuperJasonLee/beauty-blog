## Upper eyelid surgery: from double eyelid to periorbital rejuvenation

Upper eyelid surgery is the most fundamental and highest-demand procedure in eye plastic surgery. 2026 academic literature shows that upper blepharoplasty maintains its position as the number-one facial aesthetic surgery globally[^3][^4][^5]. In Asian populations, double eyelid surgery (Asian blepharoplasty) demand is especially strong, with China, South Korea, and Japan accounting for over 80% of the global Asian eyelid surgery market.

The current clinical consensus has shifted from early-era "European-style parallel crease" aesthetics toward "natural fan-shaped, personalized design" principles. Pre-operative design emphasizes comprehensive assessment of the patient's brow-eye distance, tarsal plate width, skin laxity, and orbital fat volume, rather than applying a fixed template[^4][^5]. The choice between incisional and suture techniques has also evolved in 2026: for patients with significant skin laxity or orbital fat herniation, the incisional technique remains the first choice; for young patients with thin skin and no significant laxity, modified suture techniques (continuous suture, Park method) are regaining popularity due to faster recovery and reversibility.

## Ptosis repair: precision and long-term stability

Ptosis repair is one of the most technically demanding subspecialties in eye plastic surgery. 2026 academic literature shows that levator aponeurosis repair and frontalis sling remain the two mainstream techniques, but surgical approaches continue to evolve toward greater precision and minimal invasiveness.

Levator aponeurosis repair is indicated for mild-to-moderate ptosis patients with good levator function, requiring precise intraoperative measurement of advancement amount and tarsal fixation position. 2025-2026 research focuses on standardizing intraoperative adjustment techniques - using awake testing and symmetry assessment to optimize immediate results and reduce reoperation rates.

Frontalis sling is indicated for severe ptosis patients with poor levator function (<4mm), with traditional materials including autologous fascia lata, silicone rods, and Gore-Tex. A 2026 systematic review comparing long-term recurrence rates shows that autologous fascia lata has the lowest 5-year recurrence rate (approximately 8-12%) but greater surgical trauma; synthetic materials have higher recurrence rates (15-25%) but shorter operative time and less invasiveness.

## Lower eyelid surgery and periorbital rejuvenation: from bag removal to fat repositioning

Lower blepharoplasty in 2026 is undergoing a paradigm shift from simple "remove excess skin and fat" to "preserve fat, reposition, fill tear trough." Orbital fat repositioning has become the mainstream technique - redistributing herniated orbital fat into the tear trough area, eliminating both bag protrusion and tear trough depression with a single procedure[^20].

2025-2026 academic literature further refines the approach selection for lower blepharoplasty: the transconjunctival approach is indicated for young patients with good skin elasticity and isolated fat herniation, with the advantage of no external scar; the transcutaneous approach is indicated for older patients with significant skin laxity requiring excision[^20].

Epicanthoplasty continues to grow in Asian markets, particularly medial epicanthoplasty. The latest clinical consensus emphasizes preserving the medial canthal tendon and avoiding excessive skin excision to reduce post-operative scar and epicanthus recurrence risks. Lateral canthoplasty is mainly used to improve fissure length and lateral canthal angle, but long-term stability remains a point of contention.

## Regulation, safety, and industry trends

In 2026, regulatory and safety issues in the eye plastic surgery industry continue to receive attention. FDA and NMPA approvals for eye surgery devices and fillers are tightening, particularly regarding hyaluronic acid (HA) filler use in the periorbital region - due to rich vascularity and anastomosis with the ophthalmic artery, filler intravascular injection can cause blindness and other severe complications[^1][^2].

ASPS annual statistics show that eyelid surgery volume grew approximately 5% year-over-year in 2024, ranking first among all facial aesthetic surgeries[^1]. ISAPS global statistics further indicate that Asian populations account for over 40% of global eye plastic surgery demand, with double eyelid surgery and epicanthoplasty being the fastest-growing segments.

On the Chinese-language discourse side, discussions on Zhihu about "double eyelid revision" and "unsatisfactory ptosis repair outcomes" remained at high visibility through 2025-2026[^21][^22]. These discussions reflect strong demand for pre-operative expectation management - some patients are dissatisfied with initial results and require revision surgery, which is technically more demanding and costly than primary surgery.

## Frequently Asked Questions

{{< faq >}}
- **Should I choose the incisional or suture technique for double eyelid surgery?** The incisional technique is indicated for patients with skin laxity, orbital fat herniation, or desire for long-lasting results, with recovery of 1-3 months but permanent results; the suture technique is indicated for young patients with thin skin and no significant laxity, with faster recovery (1-2 weeks) but potential loosening over time[^3][^4]. Comprehensive pre-operative evaluation by the surgeon is recommended.
- **How long does recovery take after ptosis repair?** Levator aponeurosis repair swelling subsides in 2-4 weeks, but full natural appearance requires 3-6 months; frontalis sling requires a longer adaptation period (3-6 months) as the frontalis muscle learns a new contraction pattern. Early post-operative lagophthalmos may occur, requiring artificial tears and ointment to protect the cornea.
- **Will lower eyelid surgery leave scars?** The transconjunctival approach (internal) leaves no external scar, with recovery of 1-2 weeks; the transcutaneous approach (external) incision is at the lower lash line, with scars typically inconspicuous but requiring 3-6 months to fade[^20]. For patients with concurrent tear trough depression, orbital fat repositioning is recommended over simple fat excision.
- **Will epicanthoplasty leave scars?** The incision is designed within the epicanthal fold, with scars typically fading within 3-6 months. However, patients with scar-prone constitutions should exercise caution and inform the surgeon of their scar tendency[^20].
- **Can eye surgery be combined with other facial procedures?** Yes. Common combinations include upper blepharoplasty + brow lift, lower blepharoplasty + midface lift, double eyelid + epicanthoplasty. Combined surgery can reduce total recovery time and anesthesia sessions, but the surgeon should evaluate operative time and trauma tolerance[^4].
- **How much does eye plastic surgery cost?** In the Chinese market, double eyelid surgery costs approximately 5,000-20,000 RMB, ptosis repair 10,000-30,000 RMB, lower blepharoplasty 8,000-25,000 RMB, and epicanthoplasty 5,000-15,000 RMB. Revision surgery typically costs 1.5-2x the primary surgery. Costs vary by region, hospital tier, and surgeon experience.
