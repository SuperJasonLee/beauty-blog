## Key Takeaways

- Asian double-eyelid surgery (Asian blepharoplasty) is shifting from a "one-technique-fits-all" model toward **layered anatomy + orbital fat repositioning**, with "naturalness" as the dominant 2026 aesthetic keyword.
- Lower-eyelid blepharoplasty is prioritizing **orbital fat repositioning** 〞 removing herniated fat while preserving the malar bag (tear trough) 〞 as a refined technique for both bag removal and under-eye contour maintenance.
- Tear-trough correction is advancing toward **layer-specific injection protocols** and material precision (HA vs. collagen-stimulating fillers vs. fat grafting) to minimize complications such as Tyndall effect and intravascular injury.
- Endoscopic brow lift (forehead lift) is expanding its indications, forming combined procedures with upper-eyelid blepharoplasty for patients with both brow ptosis and upper-eyelid dermatochalasis.
- Post-operative complications 〞 **upper-eyelid hollowing, scar hypertrophy, and lower-eyelid scleral show** 〞 remain the leading drivers of patient decision-making, making standardized follow-up and complication-prevention protocols central topics in 2026 industry discussions.

Upper-eyelid (double-eyelid) surgery is the most widely performed oculoplastic procedure across East Asia, and the 2026 clinical discourse reveals a clear technical stratification. From a technique-evolution standpoint, **full-incision (open) double-eyelid surgery** is gradually absorbing cases previously managed with buried-thread sutures, driven by its ability to simultaneously address mild ptosis and remove herniated orbital fat. ## Lower Eyelid & Periorbital Rejuvenation: The Full-Link Protocol from Bags to Tear Trough

Lower-eyelid blepharoplasty in 2026 is centrally defined by the question: **how to remove the infraorbital fat herniation (bags) while preserving the malar bag and tear trough?** [^11] focus on the application of orbital fat repositioning techniques in lower-eyelid surgery. Moghadam and Yukizane in *Facial Plastic Surgery* describe the "malar-bag-preservation principle" 〞 repositioning part of the herniated fat over the orbital rim into the tear trough 〞 effectively correcting bags while maintaining the natural three-dimensional contour of the East Asian midface[^11].

Tear-trough (infraorbital hollow) correction, the other centerpiece of periorbital rejuvenation, faces ongoing challenges around layer precision and material selection in 2026. [^9][^10] identify injection plane, filler rheology, and overcorrection as the key determinants of complication rates. The Tyndall effect (visible filler translucency under thin eyelid skin), intravascular injury, and overfilled contours are all more likely when injection occurs in a superficial plane. Expert consensus favours injection at the **subperiosteal or deep subdermal plane** using high-molecular-weight, low-swelling HA or a collagen-stimulating filler in Asian patients 〞 for whom the aesthetic goal is "endogenous volume" rather than "architectural augmentation."

## Brow Lift and Combined Procedures: Coordinating the Brow and the Eyelid

Brow lift (forehead lift / brow ptosis correction) in 2026 is increasingly being combined with upper-eyelid blepharoplasty in single-session procedures. [^9][^10] and related clinical literature confirm that **endoscopic brow lift** 〞 involving minimal-incision release of the frontalis and brow depressor muscles 〞 is expanding its indications, particularly for patients whose apparent "excess upper-eyelid skin" is actually caused by brow ptosis and frontalis over-action rather than true dermatochalasis.

In the East Asian aesthetic patient population, brow lift also engages the **supraorbital aesthetic unit** 〞 the brow ridge and orbital rim 〞 as a holistic consideration. Not all upper-eyelid skin laxity requires incision blepharoplasty; a subset benefits from brow elevation alone. Pre-operative evaluation of brow position relative to the orbital rim, combined with dynamic frontalis assessment, is essential for determining whether the correct plan is "brow lift alone / brow lift + blepharoplasty / blepharoplasty alone." *Allure*'s 2026 trend coverage also identifies the combined brow-eyelid approach as a rising concern in both Western and Eastern aesthetic-surgery practice[^1].

[^3][^4] affirm that botulinum toxin for dynamic forehead lines has reached a stable clinical-evidence base, while radiofrequency and microneedling-RF data for periorbital skin tightening continue to accumulate. In Chinese aesthetic clinics, the combined radiofrequency-photorejuvenation (IPL + RF) protocol has become a common entry-level offering for younger patients seeking maintenance rather than surgical intervention.

On the regulatory side, FDA communications on filler indications and device safety for periorbital injection continue to update, emphasizing operator training, informed consent, and product traceability. ## Frequently Asked Questions

{{< faq >}}
- **Should I choose buried-thread (suture) or full-incision double-eyelid surgery?** [^3][^4] recommend buried-thread for patients with thin upper-eyelid skin, minimal fat herniation, and no ptosis; full-incision blepharoplasty offers better long-term stability for patients with skin redundancy, orbital fat prolapse, or mild ptosis. A pre-operative assessment by a board-certified plastic surgeon 〞 including eyelid skin laxity, palpebral fissure width, and levator function 〞 is essential before deciding.
- **How long does recovery take after double-eyelid surgery, and when is the final shape stable?** Buried-thread typically reaches 80% edema resolution by 3 weeks and shape stability at 3每6 months. Full-incision blepharoplasty shows visible edema reduction at 2每4 weeks, with full recovery at 3每6 months and final shape evaluation recommended at 6每12 months. Avoid rubbing or strenuous exercise during recovery, and follow your surgeon's scar-management protocol.
- **Can lower-eyelid bag removal preserve the malar bag (the "eye smile")?** [^11] confirm that orbital fat repositioning 〞 moving herniated fat over the orbital rim rather than excising it 〞 enables bag removal while preserving the natural malar volume for patients who wish to maintain their characteristic under-eye contour. The surgeon must be familiar with lower-eyelid orbital anatomy and clearly define the preservation zone during pre-operative marking.
- **Is tear-trough filler safe? How can the Tyndall effect be avoided?** Tear-trough safety depends critically on injection plane precision 〞 subperiosteal or deep subdermal injection is preferred over superficial plane injection to prevent visible filler translucency (Tyndall effect). High-molecular-weight, low-swelling HA or collagen-stimulating fillers are generally preferred in Asian patients. Any visible bluish discoloration or skin transparency after injection warrants immediate re-evaluation for possible intravascular injury.
- **Can brow lift and double-eyelid surgery be done together?** Brow ptosis combined with upper-eyelid dermatochalasis is a valid combined-procedure indication[^9][^10]. Both procedures can typically be completed in one session, with overlapping recovery timelines. Patients should discuss their full aesthetic goals 〞 including long-term brow-eyelid proportion 〞 during pre-operative consultation.
- **What should I do if I have upper-eyelid hollowing or scar hypertrophy after blepharoplasty?** Upper-eyelid hollowing (often caused by excessive fat removal) may be addressed with autologous fat grafting or orbital fat repositioning revision. Scar hypertrophy responds to silicone sheeting, intralesional corticosteroid injection, or laser-assisted treatment. Both conditions warrant early (within 3 months post-op) reassessment by a surgeon experienced in revision blepharoplasty.
